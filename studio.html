Muscle Memory Musik Studio

Founded in 2005, Muscle Memory Musik Studio has grown from a hands-on creative space to a thriving artist development hub. From live recording to producing full albums, we support artists at all stages of their journey.

Studio History

2005

Muscle Memory Musik Studio is founded and built from the ground up.

Early Foundations

Producer Young Max becomes a pivotal early collaborator shaping the studio’s early sound.

2012

Dishy Tangent is formed by Reason and Matt Shilk, exploring live-led, genre-busting music.

2014

Sing Out Loud Sundays is founded by RM Moses. The studio becomes the HQ, hosting rehearsals and filmed sessions.

2019

New collaborations with Swayla and other artists mark a modern phase in UKG and electronic music.

Present

The Ladder Records collective emerges from the studio, formalizing years of collaboration and mentorship.

Dishy Tangent

Dishy Tangent, formed in 2012 by Reason and Matt Shilk, blends live instrumentation with modern production. Recent releases include Clear (2021) and W.I.L.D. (2022).
